    What can a HELOC help you do?

    Learn about how a home equity line of credit works and how it may help you realize your goals – from covering unexpected expenses to paying for educational costs and funding home renovations. adatext

    A home equity line of credit, or HELOC, could help you achieve your life priorities. At Bank of America, we want to help you understand how you might put a HELOC to work for you.

    A HELOC is a line of credit borrowed against the available equity of your home. Your home's equity is the difference between the appraised value of your home and your current mortgage balance.

    On screen copy:
    HELOC
    a line of credit borrowed against the available equity of your home
    Value of home
    Mortgage balance
    Home's Equity

    Through Bank of America, you can generally borrow up to 85% of the value of your home minus the amount you still owe.

    On screen copy:
    Value of home
    85%
    Mortgage balance
    HELOC

    For example, say your home's appraised value is $200,000. 85% of that is $170,000. If you still owe $120,000 on your mortgage, you'll subtract that, leaving you with the maximum home equity line of credit you could receive as $50,000.

    [Math equation shown on screen as it is being described in the voiceover.]

    On screen copy:

    x85%

    Much like a credit card, a HELOC is a revolving credit line that you pay down, and you only pay interest on the portion of the line you use.

    On screen copy:
    Available Credit
    Withdraw funds
    Make a payment

    With a Bank of America HELOC, there are no closing costs, no application fees, no annual fees, and no fees to use the funds.

    Plus, Bank of America offers rate discounts when you sign up for automatic payments, as well as discounts based on the funds you initially use when opening the HELOC.

    On screen copy:
    Discounts available
    Automatic Payments
    Opening Funds

    The interest rate is often lower than other forms of credit, and the interest you pay may be tax deductible, but you should consult a tax advisor.

    On screen disclosure:
    Please consult your tax advisor regarding interest deductibility as tax rules may have changed.

    Most HELOCs have a variable rate, which means the interest rate can change over time based on the Wall Street Journal Prime Rate.

    [Calendar shows an example interest rate of 5.6% and the next month it changes to 6.3%. A line graph is then shown, also demonstrating that rates can fluctuate.]

    On screen disclosure:
    Chart for illustrative purposes only.

    And Bank of America offers you the option to convert $5,000 or more of your balance to a fixed rate, so you can take advantage of fixed monthly payments and protect yourself from rising interest rates.

    On screen copy:
    $5000 or more of your balance
    Fixed rate
    Predictable monthly payments

    Continue to use your home equity line of credit as needed for the duration of your borrowing period, usually 10 years.
